Dream Educate Change

KVM assists young people to live their dreams by providing them a valuable tool — an English education, which ultimately gives them the power to change their future.

Dream

The poverty cycle in India is very apparent; especially in small cities like Visakhapatnam. The idea that "I will be poor because my parents were poor" is something that is generationally engraved in the minds of young people here. Children have either forgotten or never learned how to dream, and the few who do, only dream of getting a job. Forget dreaming of becoming a dancer or an actor. Survival is life for many of them. We do more than just teach English; we inspire our students to raise their sights and to see themselves in a larger world of possibilities. This is where change begins — a dream.

Educate

Needed essentials of clothes and hygiene kits “feed [people] for a day.” Research shows that "giving" things away in 3rd world countries as an act of humanitarian relief backfires without education. Communities have gotten used to these "free" gifts and simply wait for the next batch to come. Education is earned through KVM. Students are qualified for the program and are required to contribute funds for their education. KVM funds are supplemental scholarships. Parallel to our English curriculum, students are taught life lesson exercises that enable them to see options in their lives with which they will construct their own future.

Change

We are looking for chain breakers; young people who will change the direction of succeeding generations. This standard applies for both our students and our field volunteers. We endeavor to provide an unforgettable experience for our field volunteers whose focus is to measurably change the lives of the young people they teach. We inspire our students and give them the tools they need, to make the changes in their lives they want to see. Our mission for KVM Graduates is not only their success in the world, but also to expand KVM’s borders and enable them to assist teaching succeeding classes of students.

WHO DO WE TEACH?


We are currently teaching in a small city on the mid-eastern coast of India called Visakhapatnam in the state of Andhra Pradesh.

Within this region, we reach out to two different groups:

Graduating College Students

High School Students


KVM American volunteers teach English to two tracks of students: High School students and college students. The High School student track is a charitable arm of KVM that teaches novice English ability students. The college student track interviews and selects students with ambition, desire and basic English knowledge that upon graduation, places them into their chosen profession or improved employment.
